The Problem

Privacy is broken.
You're going to fix it.

Every digital transaction leaves a trail. Donations, payments, transfers — all permanently recorded, linkable, and exploitable. The world needs engineers who can build systems where financial privacy is a guarantee, not a privilege. That's what Ghost Protocol is about.

🔍 On-chain Surveillance

Public blockchains expose every transaction. Anyone can trace your wallet history, link your identity, and map your financial behaviour across time.

🏦 KYC Overreach

Traditional payment rails demand identity verification even for micro-donations, creating permanent records tied to sensitive causes and individuals.

🕵️ Metadata Leakage

Even when names are hidden, timestamps, amounts, and frequency patterns reveal detailed behavioural profiles that can be used against you.

💀 Wallet Persistence

Reusing wallets across transactions creates a permanent, linkable identity graph. Once linked, it never disappears — even if you want it to.

The Challenge

Build a privacy protocol.
From scratch.

Your mission is to design and implement a Zero-Knowledge micro-donation application — a system where a donor can prove they made a valid donation without revealing their identity, the amount, or the recipient. You will build the ZK circuits, the privacy protocol, and a working interface. This is not a tutorial project. This is real engineering.

🔐

ZK-Proof Circuits

Design and implement Zero-Knowledge proof circuits using Rust or your preferred ZK framework (Circom, Noir, RISC Zero). Prove validity without revealing data.

🔒

Privacy Protocol Design

Architect a protocol that handles commitment schemes, nullifiers, and merkle proofs. Your protocol must be formally sound — not just functional.

💨

Disposable Identity Layer

Implement one-time wallet generation and destruction. No persistent identity. No linkability between transactions. No trace after execution.

🌐

Working Application

Ship a functional end-to-end application — not just a whitepaper. Users must be able to donate privately through your interface. Demo it. Defend it.

Judging Criteria

How you'll be evaluated.

Judging follows a full academic presentation standard — think final year project defence, but with real money on the line. Every round is scored out of 100.

🧠

Technical Depth (30pts)

How well do you understand the cryptography behind your solution? Can you explain your ZK circuits, your protocol choices, and your security assumptions?

⚙️

Implementation Quality (25pts)

Is your code clean, well-structured, and actually working? GitHub repo quality, test coverage, and code organisation all count.

🎯

Innovation & Originality (20pts)

Did you go beyond the basics? Novel protocol design, creative privacy mechanisms, or unique application of ZK proofs will be rewarded.

🎤

Presentation & Defence (15pts)

Can you present your work clearly and defend it under questioning? Appearance, communication, and confidence matter — just like a project defence.

🖥️

Working Demo (10pts)

A live, functional demo submitted via YouTube. The system must work end-to-end. A broken demo costs you these points entirely.

FAQ

Questions answered.

Do I need to know ZK proofs before joining?

No prior ZK experience is required to register. However, you will need to learn and apply ZK concepts during the hackathon. We recommend starting with Circom or Noir if you're new to ZK circuits.

What programming languages can I use?

Rust is strongly recommended for ZK circuit development. For the application layer, you can use any stack you're comfortable with — JavaScript, Python, Go, etc. ZK frameworks like Circom, Noir, and RISC Zero are all accepted.

What do I submit each round?

Each round requires a PDF report (progress documentation), a GitHub repository link (your code), and a YouTube demo video showing your working implementation. All three are required for a complete submission.

Can I change my team members after registering?

Team composition is fixed after registration. Choose your teammates carefully before signing up. The team leader registers and shares the Team Code for others to join.

How does the Team Code work?

When a team leader registers, a unique Team Code is generated on their dashboard. They share this code with teammates. Each teammate registers separately, then enters the code on their dashboard to join the team.

Is the registration fee refundable?

Registration fees are non-refundable. Once payment is confirmed, your slot is secured for the full 31-day hackathon.

How are prizes paid out?

Prize money is paid directly to winners via bank transfer after the final round is scored and results are verified by FLINN Software Labs. Winners will be contacted within 7 days of the final round closing.

What if my payment went through but my dashboard is still locked?

Go to your pending payment page and click "Check Status". If it's still locked after a few minutes, contact FLINN Software Labs with your Paystack payment reference and we'll resolve it manually.

Can university students from any school participate?

Yes. Ghost Protocol is open to all Nigerian university students and recent graduates. You do not need to be from a specific institution.

What happens if I miss a round submission?

Missing a round means a score of zero for that round. Your cumulative score across all rounds determines your final ranking. You can still submit in subsequent rounds.
