The Problem

Terrified of digital
paper trails?

Every donation you make leaves a fingerprint. Wallets, IP addresses, transaction graphs — surveillance capitalism has turned generosity into a liability.

🔍 On-chain Surveillance

Public blockchains expose every transaction. Anyone can trace your wallet history, link your identity, and map your causes.

🏦 KYC Overreach

Traditional payment rails demand identity verification even for micro-donations, creating permanent records tied to sensitive causes.

🕵️ Metadata Leakage

Timestamps, amounts, and frequency patterns reveal behavioral profiles even when names are hidden.

💀 Wallet Persistence

Reusing wallets across transactions creates a permanent, linkable identity graph that never disappears.

The Hack

Verified. Anonymous.
Untraceable.

Build the infrastructure that lets anyone donate to any cause — provably, privately, permanently.

🔐

ZK-Proof Verification

Prove you made a valid donation without revealing who you are, how much, or to whom. Zero-knowledge, full integrity.

💨

Self-Destructing Wallets

Disposable one-time wallets that auto-destruct after a single transaction. No history. No linkability. No trace.

🌐

Decentralized Routing

Funds route through a privacy-preserving mixer protocol. Amounts are split, delayed, and re-assembled at destination.

🛡️

Identity Firewall

Cryptographic commitments replace personal data. The system knows you're real — it just doesn't know you're you.
